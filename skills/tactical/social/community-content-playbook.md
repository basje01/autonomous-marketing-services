## Context

Use this when a brand has an active community but treats it as a support channel or announcement board instead of a content engine and growth lever. Communities that only receive broadcasts lose members. Communities that amplify member voices retain members and generate organic content that outperforms brand-produced content on social platforms.

**Community-generated content outperforms brand content on every engagement metric.** UGC posts receive 28% higher engagement than branded posts on Instagram (Stackla research). The reason is trust - people trust other people more than they trust brands. A community member saying "I love this product" is more persuasive than the brand saying "Our product is great."

**Community is a flywheel, not a channel.** The community produces content (UGC, discussions, memes). You amplify that content on social. Social reach attracts new community members. New members produce more content. Each rotation of the flywheel accelerates the next. But the flywheel only works if you actively amplify member contributions - passive communities decay.

**Engagement rituals create habit loops.** A weekly community event (AMAs, challenges, themed days) gives members a reason to return on a predictable schedule. Without rituals, community engagement is sporadic - members visit when they remember, which becomes less frequent over time until they lapse entirely.

## Procedure

1. Audit the current community. Measure: daily active members (DAM), messages per day, content creation rate (posts with images/videos vs. text-only), member retention (30-day return rate), and lurker-to-contributor ratio (typically 90/9/1 - 90% lurk, 9% contribute occasionally, 1% create regularly). This baseline determines the playbook's intensity.
2. Identify community member segments:
   - **Creators (top 1-5%):** Regular content producers - memes, art, tutorials, reviews. These are your content partners.
   - **Contributors (5-15%):** Regular participants in discussions, Q&A, event attendance. They generate engagement but not standalone content.
   - **Lurkers (80-90%):** Read but don't post. The goal is to convert 5-10% of lurkers into contributors through low-barrier engagement opportunities.
3. Design the UGC pipeline:
   - **Source:** Community challenges, themed prompts, product-in-use requests, member stories
   - **Permission:** Clear opt-in mechanism (reaction to agree, form submission, or community rule stating shared content may be amplified)
   - **Curation:** Select the best 3-5 pieces per week for social amplification
   - **Attribution:** Always credit the creator by name/handle, tag them, and ask permission for each specific use
   - **Incentive:** Recognition (feature, shoutout) > rewards (merch, credits) > payment. Recognition is the most sustainable motivator.
4. Build the engagement ritual calendar. Each ritual needs: a name, a frequency, a format, and a responsible moderator. Examples:
   - Weekly: "Show & Tell" (members share their setup/collection/use case), themed discussion thread
   - Bi-weekly: AMA with team member or community expert
   - Monthly: Community challenge with a theme (best photo, creative interpretation, tutorial)
   - Quarterly: Milestone celebration, community retrospective, member spotlight compilation
5. Design the member recognition system. Recognition must be merit-based and visible:
   - Shoutouts for top contributors (weekly or bi-weekly)
   - Member spotlight (feature a creator's story monthly)
   - Community milestones (celebrate member counts, content milestones, achievement unlocks genuinely)
   - Roles or badges for sustained contribution (but don't inflate - too many badges devalue all of them)
6. Build the community-to-social pipeline. The process for turning community content into social posts:
   - Monday: Review community content from the past week
   - Tuesday: Select top 3-5 pieces, request permission if not pre-approved
   - Wednesday-Friday: Publish 2-3 community-sourced posts with full attribution
   - Track performance: community-sourced posts vs. brand-produced posts (engagement rate, reach)

## QA Rubric (scored)

Each dimension scored 0-5. A passing file scores 16/20 minimum.

| Dimension | 0–1 | 2–3 | 4–5 |
|-----------|-----|-----|-----|
| UGC pipeline quality | No UGC sourcing process; community content is not used on social channels | UGC is occasionally shared but with no systematic sourcing, permission process, or attribution | Structured UGC pipeline with weekly cadence, explicit permission mechanism, consistent attribution, and curation criteria; community-to-social flow is documented and repeatable |
| Engagement ritual design | No recurring events; community interaction is ad-hoc; no scheduled touchpoints | Some events exist but frequency is inconsistent; no named moderator; events feel like broadcasts not conversations | Weekly, bi-weekly, and monthly rituals with named moderators, specific formats, and clear goals; rituals are designed to activate lurkers and celebrate contributors |
| Recognition authenticity | No member recognition; or generic "thanks for being here" messages that feel hollow | Recognition exists but is arbitrary (random selection) or infrequent (annual); no visible system | Merit-based recognition with clear criteria, visible badges/roles, weekly shoutouts, and monthly spotlights; recognition is public and celebrated; criteria are transparent |
| Sustainability | Playbook requires 20+ hours/week of moderation with 1 person; burnout within 2 weeks | Workload is reasonable but no contingency for busy weeks; no delegation plan | Rituals and cadence matched to available moderation capacity; includes delegation plan; batch processing built in; sustainable for 12+ weeks at current team size |

## Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Correct Approach |
|---|---|---|
| Treating community as a broadcast channel | One-way announcements ("Check out our new product!") without engagement prompts or member content. Members feel like an audience, not a community. Engagement drops, lurker ratio climbs to 95%+ | Design every community interaction to invite response: polls, questions, challenges, member features. The community should talk to each other, not just listen to the brand |
| Using UGC without permission or attribution | Resharing a member's photo without asking or crediting them. Even if community rules "allow" it, uncredited use feels extractive. The most prolific creators stop sharing because they don't get recognized | Always ask before specific reposts. Always attribute by name/handle. Feature the creator, not just the content. Make permission and attribution the default, not the exception |
| Manufactured milestones and fake celebration | "We hit 1,000 members!" when 950 are bots or inactive. Or celebrating milestones so frequently that each one feels meaningless. Manufactured excitement erodes authenticity | Celebrate genuine milestones with context: "We hit 500 active daily members - here's what this community built this month." Pair the milestone with member stories or impact data. Quality over frequency |
| Engagement rituals without a moderator | "We'll do a weekly AMA!" - but nobody is assigned to plan, moderate, or follow up. The first AMA is great. The second is okay. The third doesn't happen. The community learns that promises are hollow | Every ritual needs a named moderator with a time commitment. If nobody can commit, reduce the frequency. One well-run monthly event beats four abandoned weekly ones |

## Examples (good/bad)

**Good:** Kenzo/APED community playbook. Discord (2,400 members, 340 DAM). Weekly "PFP Show & Tell" - members share their AI-generated PFPs, top 3 featured on Twitter with full credit. Bi-weekly AMA with the art team. Monthly challenge: "Reimagine your PFP in [style]" - winner gets a custom generation. UGC pipeline: Monday review, Tuesday permissions, Wed-Fri social posts. Lurker-to-contributor conversion target: 8% → 12%.

**Bad:** "We post announcements in Discord once a week and nobody responds." - one-way broadcast, no rituals, no UGC pipeline, no member recognition, no engagement mechanics.

## Variants

- **Crypto/web3 variant:** Community IS the product. Primary platforms: Discord + Telegram + Twitter. Content mix: 50% member-generated memes and art, 30% market commentary and alpha, 20% product updates. Governance participation as a form of engagement. Token-gated channels for power users.
- **B2B / SaaS variant:** Community is a retention and expansion lever. Platform: Slack, Circle, or dedicated forum. Content focus: peer-to-peer support, use-case sharing, feature requests, expert AMAs. Member recognition: "Power User" program with early access, feedback channels, and conference invitations.
- **Creator economy variant:** Community members ARE the content creators. Platform: Discord + YouTube + TikTok. UGC is the primary social content strategy. Focus on enabling creation (templates, tools, tutorials) rather than curating it. Revenue sharing or commission programs for top creators.
- **48h sprint:** Start one weekly engagement ritual on the primary platform. Example: "Friday Show & Tell." Promote it once, run it once, measure participation. If > 5% of active members participate, continue and add a second ritual in week 3. If < 5%, adjust format or timing before adding complexity.
