## Context

Use this when a brand wants to partner with influencers or creators but has no structured process for finding, vetting, or briefing them. The most common influencer marketing failure is choosing partners based on follower count rather than audience alignment - resulting in reach without relevance.

**Audience fit trumps follower count.** A micro-influencer (10K-50K followers) in your exact niche with 5% engagement rate will drive more conversions than a macro-influencer (500K+ followers) in a tangential niche with 1% engagement. The micro-influencer's audience is your target customer. The macro-influencer's audience includes 95% of people who will never buy your product.

**Fake followers are endemic.** Studies estimate 10-25% of influencer followers are bots or inactive accounts. An influencer with 100K followers and 20K fakes has an effective audience of 80K - and their engagement rate is artificially deflated. Always run an audience authenticity check before committing budget.

**Creative freedom produces better content.** Brands that dictate every word of an influencer's post get content that sounds like an ad. Audiences have trained themselves to skip ad-sounding content. A content brief should set guardrails (brand messages, disclosure requirements, no-go topics) and let the creator execute in their voice. Creator-authentic content outperforms brand-scripted content by 2-3x on engagement.

## Procedure

1. Define the ideal influencer profile. Based on your target audience: what content categories do they follow? What platforms do they spend time on? What tone and aesthetic resonates with them? Create a profile: platform, niche/category, follower range, engagement rate minimum, geographic relevance, content style, and audience demographics that must overlap with your target customer.
2. Source candidates. Methods: platform native search (hashtags, explore pages, "for you" feeds), influencer databases (CreatorIQ, Upfluence, AspireIQ), competitor analysis (who are your competitors' customers following?), customer surveys ("whose content do you follow?"), and manual discovery (industry events, podcasts, newsletters). Target 15-20 candidates to shortlist down to 5-10.
3. Vet each candidate with a fit score. Score each candidate (1-5) on:
   - **Audience alignment:** Does their audience demographics (age, gender, location, interests) match your target customer? 5 = exact match, 1 = no overlap.
   - **Engagement rate:** Compare to tier benchmark (nano 5-8%, micro 3-5%, mid 2-3%, macro 1-2%). 5 = above benchmark, 1 = below benchmark.
   - **Content quality:** Is their content production quality consistent with your brand standards? 5 = exceeds, 1 = well below.
   - **Audience authenticity:** What percentage of followers appear genuine? Use tools (HypeAuditor, Social Blade) or manual checks (comment quality, follower growth pattern). 5 = 95%+ real, 1 = significant bot presence.
   - **Brand safety:** Any controversial content, competing brand partnerships, or value misalignment? 5 = fully aligned, 1 = red flags.
   Total fit score out of 25. Shortlist: 18+ out of 25.
4. Define the compensation model. Standard approaches by tier:
   - **Nano (1K-10K):** Product gifting + small fee ($50-$250 per post)
   - **Micro (10K-50K):** Fee per deliverable ($250-$1,000 per post)
   - **Mid (50K-200K):** Fee per deliverable ($1,000-$5,000 per post)
   - **Macro (200K+):** Negotiated rate ($5,000+) or performance-based (affiliate commission)
   Always include usage rights in the compensation discussion - rights to reuse content in paid ads costs extra.
5. Write the content brief. The brief should include: campaign overview (one paragraph), key messages (2-3 points to mention), content requirements (format, length, platform), brand guardrails (no-go topics, competitor mentions, tone boundaries), disclosure requirements (FTC: #ad or #sponsored, visible and unambiguous), and creative freedom statement ("We trust your voice - here's the message, how you deliver it is up to you").
6. Draft the outreach email. The email should: (a) demonstrate you've actually consumed their content (reference a specific recent post), (b) explain why they're a fit (specific reason, not generic flattery), (c) state the ask clearly (what you want, what you'll provide), (d) include compensation range upfront (don't make them ask), and (e) keep it under 200 words.

## QA Rubric (scored)

Each dimension scored 0-5. A passing file scores 16/20 minimum.

| Dimension | 0–1 | 2–3 | 4–5 |
|-----------|-----|-----|-----|
| Selection rigor | Influencers chosen by follower count alone; no engagement rate calculation; no audience authenticity check | Engagement rate calculated but no audience authenticity check; audience alignment assumed from niche, not verified | 5-dimension fit score (alignment, engagement, quality, authenticity, safety); audience authenticity checked with tools or manual review; benchmarked against tier averages |
| Compensation fairness | "We'll pay you in exposure" or no compensation discussed; or rates significantly below market for the tier | Compensation exists but is below market or doesn't include usage rights discussion | Fair compensation benchmarked to tier; usage rights explicitly discussed and compensated; deliverables clearly defined; payment terms transparent |
| Brief quality | Brief dictates every word; no creative freedom; or no brief at all (just "promote our product") | Brief includes key messages but is overly prescriptive about format and tone; or too vague with no brand guardrails | Key messages clear; format requirements specified; brand guardrails set; explicit creative freedom statement; disclosure requirements included; brief respects the creator's voice |
| Outreach quality | Generic mass email ("Dear Influencer, we love your content"); no specific reference to their work; no compensation mentioned | Personalized but compensation not mentioned upfront; or too long (>300 words); or doesn't explain why they specifically are a fit | References specific recent content; explains why they're a fit (not generic flattery); states compensation range upfront; under 200 words; clear next step |

## Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Correct Approach |
|---|---|---|
| Choosing influencers by follower count | A 500K-follower lifestyle account has 5% audience overlap with your artisanal homeware brand. You pay $5,000 for a post that reaches 475K people who will never buy your product. Cost per relevant impression: $0.21. A 15K-follower interior design account with 80% overlap costs $500 and reaches 12K relevant people. Cost per relevant impression: $0.04 | Score audience alignment as the primary selection criterion. Use demographics overlap, not niche assumption. A smaller, perfectly aligned audience > a larger, tangentially aligned one |
| "We'll pay you in exposure" | Creators are professionals. Asking for free work signals the brand doesn't value their craft. The creators who accept "exposure" deals are the ones who can't command fees - and their content quality reflects that | Pay fair market rates for the tier. Budget too small for paid partnerships? Partner with nano-influencers ($50-$250 + product) or run a product-seeding program instead of asking for free work |
| Dictating every word of the post | The post sounds like a press release. The audience recognizes it immediately as sponsored content and scrolls past. The creator's engagement rate on sponsored posts drops 40-60% compared to organic because the content doesn't match their voice | Set brand guardrails (key messages, disclosure, no-go topics) and let the creator execute in their voice. Review for accuracy and compliance, not for tone or creative choices. Trust is why you chose them |
| No FTC/ASA disclosure requirements in the brief | The influencer posts without #ad or #sponsored. The FTC investigates. The brand receives a warning letter and potential fine. The influencer's audience feels deceived | Include disclosure requirements explicitly in every content brief: "#ad or #sponsored required, visible in first line of caption / first 3 seconds of video." This protects the brand, the creator, and the audience |

## Examples (good/bad)

**Good:** Ashy & Sleek influencer brief for Instagram. Target: micro-influencers (10-30K) in interior design / home decor niche, Netherlands and Belgium, engagement rate > 3.5%. Shortlist: 8 creators scored on 5 dimensions, top scorer 22/25 (@interieur_van_anna, 18K followers, 4.8% ER, 96% audience authenticity, content style perfectly matches artisanal aesthetic). Compensation: EUR 400 per feed post + 2 stories + product gifted (EUR 180 value). Content brief: "Show our products in your home. Key messages: handcrafted, sustainable materials, designed in Brabant. Creative execution is yours." Disclosure: "#ad required, first line of caption."

**Bad:** "DM 50 influencers with 100K+ followers: 'Hey we love your content! Want to promote our product for free?'" - no vetting, no audience analysis, no compensation, no brief, no strategy.

## Variants

- **Crypto/web3 variant (KOLs):** Platform: Twitter/X. Selection criteria emphasize: crypto-native audience, discussion depth (threads > one-liners), credibility in the specific niche (DeFi, NFTs, memecoin). Compensation: often token-based or advisory equity alongside cash. Disclosure: crypto promotion regulations vary by jurisdiction - include legal review step.
- **B2B variant:** Platform: LinkedIn. "Influencers" are industry thought leaders, analysts, or practitioners. Compensation often non-monetary: co-authorship, event invitations, product advisory board seat, exclusive data access. Content: long-form posts, case studies, or co-created reports rather than product reviews.
- **Product seeding variant (no paid posts):** Send product to 20-50 creators with no posting obligation. Track which creators naturally post about it. Those who do are candidates for paid partnerships. Cost: product COGS + shipping. Risk: low. Signal: a creator who posts unprompted genuinely likes your product - their future sponsored content will be more authentic.
- **48h sprint:** Identify 5 potential influencers using one method (hashtag search on target platform). Score each on audience alignment and engagement rate only (skip authenticity and brand safety for now). Draft one outreach email. Send to the top-scoring candidate. Full vetting for remaining candidates in week 2 if the first partnership shows promise.
