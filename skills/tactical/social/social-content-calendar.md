## Context

Use this when social media posting is ad-hoc, inconsistent, or relies on cross-posting the same content everywhere. A content calendar is not just a scheduling tool - it is an editorial strategy that ensures the right content reaches the right audience on the right platform at the right time.

**Cross-posting kills engagement.** A LinkedIn post that reads like a tweet is too informal for the audience. A tweet that reads like a LinkedIn post is too formal and long. Instagram demands visual-first thinking. Each platform's algorithm rewards content that matches its native format - cross-posted content is systematically suppressed because it generates lower engagement signals.

**The first hour determines reach.** Social algorithms amplify posts that generate early engagement. A post that gets 10 comments in the first hour reaches 5-10x more people than a post that gets 10 comments over 24 hours. The first-hour engagement plan (who you tag, which communities you share with, how you respond to early comments) is as important as the content itself.

**70/30 is the minimum value-to-promotion ratio.** Audiences follow brands that teach, entertain, or inspire - not brands that constantly pitch. For every promotional post, publish at least 2-3 value-giving posts. Value-giving includes: educational content, behind-the-scenes, community spotlights, industry commentary, and user-generated content amplification.

## Procedure

1. Define content pillars (3-5 recurring themes). Each pillar should align with brand positioning and audience interests. Example for a design agency: (1) design process behind-the-scenes, (2) industry trends and commentary, (3) client case studies, (4) team culture, (5) educational tips. Pillars ensure variety while maintaining brand coherence.
2. Set platform-specific posting cadence based on available resources:
   - Twitter/X: 3-7 posts/week (high volume, short shelf life)
   - LinkedIn: 2-4 posts/week (lower volume, longer shelf life, professional tone)
   - Instagram: 3-5 feed posts/week + 5-7 stories/week (visual-first, stories have 24h life)
   - TikTok: 3-5 videos/week (algorithm rewards consistency and volume)
   Reduce cadence if resources are thin - 2 quality posts/week beats 5 low-effort posts/week.
3. Define the content mix per platform. Assign a percentage to each content type:
   - Educational / value-giving: 40-50%
   - Engagement / community: 20-30%
   - Promotional / product: 15-25%
   - Culture / behind-the-scenes: 10-15%
   Total must equal 100%. Promotional must not exceed 30%.
4. Build the 4-week calendar at the post level. For each post: date, time, platform, content pillar, format (text, image, video, carousel, thread, story), caption/copy direction, visual direction, CTA, and objective (engage, educate, convert, amplify).
5. Write the first-hour engagement plan per platform:
   - Who are the 5-10 accounts to tag or mention in posts?
   - Which communities, groups, or chats should the post be shared in?
   - Who on the team responds to comments in the first hour?
   - What is the response protocol (reply to every comment? Like all? Ask follow-up questions?)
6. Define performance metrics per platform. Not vanity metrics - engagement rate (interactions / reach), click-through rate (for link posts), follower growth rate, and content-specific metrics (saves on Instagram, quote tweets on X, comments on LinkedIn).

## QA Rubric (scored)

Each dimension scored 0-5. A passing file scores 16/20 minimum.

| Dimension | 0–1 | 2–3 | 4–5 |
|-----------|-----|-----|-----|
| Platform-native quality | Same content copy-pasted across platforms; no format or tone adaptation | Content is adapted for length but not for platform-specific format (e.g., no threads for X, no carousels for LinkedIn, no stories for Instagram) | Every post is designed for the platform's native format, tone, and engagement mechanics; formats match platform strengths; tone matches audience expectations |
| Content mix discipline | No explicit ratio; posting is ad-hoc or all promotional; no content pillars | Content pillars exist but mix ratio is not defined or promotional exceeds 30% | 3-5 content pillars with explicit percentage allocation; promotional content capped at 25-30%; each post tagged to a pillar and objective |
| First-hour engagement | No engagement plan; posts are published and forgotten; comments go unanswered for hours | Engagement is mentioned but no specific plan (no tag list, no response owner, no protocol) | Named response owner, specific tag list (5-10 accounts), community share targets, response protocol with timing commitment; engagement plan is as detailed as the content plan |
| Sustainability | Calendar requires 10+ posts/week with a one-person team; burnout guaranteed within 2 weeks | Cadence is reasonable but no contingency for low-resource weeks or creator block | Cadence explicitly matched to available resources; includes "low-effort" post templates for busy weeks; batch creation recommendations; posting schedule is sustainable for 12+ weeks |

## Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Why It Fails | Correct Approach |
|---|---|---|
| Cross-posting identical content to all platforms | LinkedIn's algorithm suppresses content with low engagement signals. A tweet-length post on LinkedIn generates fewer comments than a LinkedIn-native post. Same content = lower engagement per platform = lower reach = wasted effort | Write platform-native content from scratch. A thread on X, a carousel on LinkedIn, a story on Instagram. Start from the platform's constraints and audience expectations |
| 100% promotional content | Audiences unfollow brands that only pitch. Social media is a relationship channel, not a billboard. Promotional-only feeds generate unfollow rates of 2-5% per month | Maintain 70/30+ value-to-promotion ratio. For every product post, publish 2-3 educational, community, or culture posts. Value-giving content earns the right to promote |
| Publishing and ignoring | A post gets 15 comments in the first hour. Nobody responds until the next day. The algorithm reads low reply-rate as low-quality content and suppresses future distribution. Early commenters feel ignored and stop engaging | Assign a named person to monitor each platform for the first hour after every post. Reply to every comment within 60 minutes. Ask follow-up questions. Early engagement signals amplification |
| Unsustainable posting frequency | "We'll post 3x/day on all platforms!" lasts 2 weeks before burnout. Quality drops, then frequency drops, then the account goes silent for a month. Inconsistency is worse than low frequency | Set cadence based on actual available hours. 2 quality posts/week sustained for 12 months beats 14 posts/week for 3 weeks. Include batch creation days and template posts for low-bandwidth weeks |

## Examples (good/bad)

**Good:** Kenzo/APED 4-week X calendar. Pillars: (1) community memes/UGC, (2) product updates, (3) market commentary, (4) behind-the-scenes. Mix: 45% community, 25% commentary, 20% product, 10% BTS. Cadence: 5 posts/week (Mon-Fri). First-hour plan: tag 8 community accounts, share in Telegram group, founder replies to first 10 comments within 30 minutes. Metric: engagement rate (target: 4.5% → 6.0%).

**Bad:** "Post the same press release on Twitter, LinkedIn, Instagram, and Facebook at the same time every Tuesday." - no platform adaptation, no content mix, no engagement plan, no metrics.

## Variants

- **Single-platform variant:** One platform, 4 weeks of depth. Include A/B testing of content formats (threads vs. single posts, images vs. video) to optimize format-level engagement before expanding to other platforms.
- **Community-first variant (crypto/web3):** Primary platform is Twitter/X + Discord/Telegram. Content mix inverts: 50% community/UGC, 30% commentary, 15% product, 5% culture. Community amplification replaces traditional engagement plan - arm community members with shareable content (memes, quote graphics).
- **B2B variant:** Primary platform is LinkedIn. Content mix emphasizes thought leadership (40%) and case studies (25%). Posting cadence: 3/week (Tue/Wed/Thu mornings). First-hour engagement focuses on employee amplification - team members comment and share within the first hour to boost algorithm signals.
- **48h sprint:** Build Week 1 only on the single highest-engagement platform. 3 posts with full detail (copy, visual, CTA, first-hour plan). Publish. Measure. Use performance data to calibrate Weeks 2-4.
