## SELF-EVALUATION CHECKLIST

Before delivering any output, verify:

- [ ] Visitor awareness level classified before writing?
- [ ] Messaging hierarchy exists or has been requested from content-strategist?
- [ ] Hero headline passes the "bar test" (5-12 words, plain language)?
- [ ] Every section headline makes a claim (not a label)?
- [ ] Every claim has adjacent proof or is flagged as [PLACEHOLDER: real data required]?
- [ ] CTA is verb + outcome (not "Learn More" or "Submit")?
- [ ] 3 headline variants provided with distinct hypotheses?
- [ ] Copy matches client brand voice from COMPANY CONTEXT?
- [ ] No fabricated metrics, testimonials, or conversion claims?
- [ ] Anti-patterns checklist passed -- no violations?
- [ ] All persuasion framework choices justified for page type and awareness level?
- [ ] Voice-of-Customer language reflected in copy (or flagged as unavailable)?
- [ ] Confidence level disclosed (HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW) with justification?
- [ ] Handoff block included when routing to another skill?
- [ ] Company context applied throughout (not generic advice)?
- [ ] Testing recommendations included (what to test first, expected impact)?

### Challenge Before Delivery

| Common Confident Error | Counter-Evidence | Resolution Criterion |
|----------------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Writing copy before verifying awareness level -- defaulting to Product Aware when visitor is Problem Aware | Schwartz's five levels show that copy sophistication mismatch is the #1 cause of underperforming pages; wrong level = wrong headline strategy | Awareness level explicitly classified per page with evidence (traffic source, funnel position, audience data) |
| Claiming a headline "will convert better" without A/B test data to support the prediction | Meguellati et al. (arXiv:2512.03373) showed even AI-preferred copy varies by context; Fiez et al. (arXiv:2402.10870) proved adaptive testing outperforms fixed assumptions | All conversion predictions framed as hypotheses with testing plan; no unqualified "this will work" claims |
| Applying the same CTA copy across pages with different visitor intent and readiness | Different pages serve different awareness levels; "Book a Demo" on a Problem Aware page is premature | CTA specificity matched to page context; each page gets CTA aligned to visitor readiness level |
